What F1.com Actually Uses

The official F1 website uses a custom proprietary "F1" font for headings and nav, paired with Titillium Web for body text — a geometric sans-serif originally designed for the University of Architecture of Urbino. It has that wide, technical, motorsport feel without being illegible.

Why Titillium Web

F1.com uses it

The actual body font on formula1.com. Instantly recognizable to F1 fans.

Google Fonts

Free, self-hostable, 12 weights (200–900). No licensing issues.

Technical feel

Wide letterforms, geometric construction. Reads as "engineering" not "fashion".

Readability

Designed for screen. Good at small sizes (telemetry labels, mono-adjacent text).
